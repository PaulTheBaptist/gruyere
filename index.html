Overview

Population dynamics in the style of Yodzis and Innes (1992) in R. This package depends upon Cheddar.

Video

The short video below was genereated using Gruyere. It shows two dynamical model simulations of the dataset from Tuesday Lake, Michigan, USA, sampled in 1984 (1,2,3). The graphs show log-transformed numerical abundance (N, individuals per metre cubed) against body mass (M, kg). The empirical measured N of primary producers is shown by green circles, invertebrates by blue squares and vertebrates by purple diamonds. Crosses show the model simulation population densities.

The two graphs show simulation runs for different parameter sets. The left-hand plot uses model parameters that result in good model-data agreement. The right-hand plot uses model parameters that result in poor model-data agreement.

  1. Carpenter, S. & Kitchell, J. (1996) The trophic cascade in lakes, Cambridge University Press.
  2. Jonsson, T., Cohen, J.E. & Carpenter, S.R. (2005) Food webs, body size, and species abundance in ecological community description, Advances In Ecological Research, 36, 1–84.
  3. Cohen, J., Jonsson, T. & Carpenter, S. (2003) Ecological community description using the food web, species abundance, and body size, Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America, 100, 1781–1786.
